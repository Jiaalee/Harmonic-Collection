FORTUNE
COOKIE

Fortune cookies are crisp, crescent-shaped cookies that are commonly served at the end of a meal at Chinese restaurants,

each containing a slip of paper containing a short message, a proverb, or a piece of wisdom.

While not originally Chinese, but rather an American invention, these delights have become synonymous with Chinese food in the Western world. The exterior of the cookie is a delicate balance of sweetness, while within is the surprise of a fortune, often cryptic or inspirational, providing a little pause for meditation or humor, and adding a sense of mystique and suspense to the end of a meal.

Typically, fortune cookies include a small slip of paper with a printed message. The message is inserted into the cookie while it is still warm and pliable. The fortune is then sealed within the cookie by folding it into a distinctive form.



Messages might range from general words of wisdom to hazy forecasts or lucky numbers.



The fortune is disclosed when the cookie is cracked open, providing a surprise and frequently a hint of insight or amusement to the person who receives it.
