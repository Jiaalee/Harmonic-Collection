TASSEOGRAPHY

Tasseography is an ancient kind of divination that involves the interpretation of patterns created at the bottom of a cup or saucer by tea leaves, coffee grounds, or other sediment. Practitioners would intuitively identify messages, omens, or insights about the future, events, or the querent's life by reading the shapes, symbols, or arrangements left by the residue. Tasseography is a distinctive tradition that gives seekers a glimpse into the unknown by producing seemingly random yet important patterns after drinking a cup of tea or coffee.

Tasseography evolved as a divination tradition involving readings of patterns produced by tea leaves or coffee grounds, with roots in ancient China and spreading throughout the Middle East and Europe. During its peak during the Victorian era, it was a fashionable social practice in which residue in cups was examined for symbols said to hold insights into the future or the drinker's life.
