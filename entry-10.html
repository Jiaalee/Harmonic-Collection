Bone casting is a type of divination practiced by tossing or casting bones, shells, or similar materials onto a surface and interpreting the patterns or positions that appear. Bone casting, which is often connected with numerous African, Native American, and other indigenous civilizations, entails a collection of bones, shells, stones, or curios, each with a symbolic value. Practitioners value the location, orientation, and arrangement of these elements in order to acquire insights, answers, or direction on questions about life, destiny, or specific concerns. The interpretation of the patterns made by the bones is subjective, guided by intuition and traditional knowledge passed down through generations, and provides spiritual guidance, warnings, or insights to those who seek it.

Bone throwing is one of the oldest forms of divination and can be found across multiple cultures throughout history. Because of this, there are multiple ways to practice throwing the bones and ways to interpret their meanings. Throwing bones, also called Osteomancy, is an ancient form of divination probably originated with various tribes in Africa. Throwing bones as a means of foretelling the future or divining the past might also have been used among indigenous American and South American tribes.
